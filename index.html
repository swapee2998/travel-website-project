Known as the Pink City because of the colour of the stone used exclusively in the walled city, Rajasthan's beautiful pink city Jaipur has much more to offer visitors everything from pageants and festivals to extraordinarily clad people, jaipur Architecture is world wide famous because of its technical details and beauty. You can soak your senses on the many lakes, gardens and hill stations that attract thousands of tourists every year.Jammu, the Kashmir Valley and Ladakh. Amritsar

Amritsar, literally meaning the 'Pool of the Nectar of Immortality', is one of the major cities of the Punjab state. It is home to the Harmandir Sahib (referred to as the "Golden Temple" in the western media), the spiritual and cultural center for the Sikh religion. This important Sikh shrine attracts more visitors than the Taj Mahal with more than 100,000 visitors on week days alone and is the most popular destination for Non-resident Indians (NRI) in the whole of India

Sikkim

Sikkim is sheer magic. This is not just the most beautiful place in the world but cleanest and safest too. If once the charms of the State were limited to mists, mountains and colourful butterflies, they are now complimented by tangible development and progress. With its unique culture and natural landscape, Sikkim is a picture of perfection and pristine purity. Nestled in the Himalayas and endowed with exceptional natural resources, Sikkim is a hotspot of biodiversity and development.
